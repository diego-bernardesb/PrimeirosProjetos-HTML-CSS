Who was Freddie:

Born in Zanzibar, Tanzania, the son of Bomi and Jer Bulsara, the boy started studying piano in Mumbai (India) when he was just seven years old. In 1964 Freddie Mercury's family migrated to Middlesex (London). It was at Ealing Technical College and School of Art that the young man started to study graphic design. Graduation took place in 1969.

Freddie Mercury's career:

Despite having graduated in Design, Freddie has dedicated his entire career to music.

Passionate about music, Freddie showed interest in the piano in his childhood. In addition to musical talent, the singer and composer had some peculiarities that helped him in the vocal part. Mercury had four more teeth (the incisors), legend has it that the width of the mouth helped to potentiate the singer's voice. During graduation, the boy started singing in a band called Wreckage. Roger Taylor (1949) and Brain May (1947), in turn, had a band called Smile. When the Smile frontman left, Freddie Mercury replaced him and the boys decided to change the name of the ensemble to Queen. The last member to join the group was John Deacon (1951), who joined the group in March 1971.

Main relationships:

One of the most striking people in the singer's life was Mary Austin, with whom she had a loving relationship that became a huge friendship. The two met when she worked in a clothing store, she was 19 and he was 24. A few months after they met, the two went to live together in Kesington and, in 1973, Freddie proposed to Mary. The marriage never happened because, at a certain point in the relationship, the singer assumed his bisexuality.

Where his next and last love relationship was with Jim Hutton, who stayed by his side until his last day of life.
